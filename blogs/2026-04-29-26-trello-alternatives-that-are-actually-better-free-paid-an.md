<h2><b>Paid Trello alternatives </b></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">You’ve seen the table, now let’s get into what these tools actually look like when you’re using them every day. Here are all the paid Trello alternatives.</span></p>
<h3><b>Wrike: Best for complex projects</b></h3>
<p><a href="https://www.wrike.com/"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Wrike</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> is our scalable and robust project management platform. Teams often switch from Trello to Wrike when they’re looking for a tool that can handle larger or more complex projects. </span></p>
<figure class="custom-image custom-image--outline"><img alt="Product screenshot showing board view with progress status and completed tasks overview." class="custom-image-override " height="null" src="https://web-static.wrike.com/blog/content/uploads/www_blogv3/htdocs/content/uploads/2024/11/product-screenshot-board-view-progress-completed-overview.png" width="null" /></figure>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">What really sets Wrike apart from other project management solutions is its customization. Compared to Trello, Wrike does much more than just Kanban boards (</span><a href="https://www.wrike.com/features/kanban-boards/"><span style="font-weight: 400;">although we have those too</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">). </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Instead, no matter how you want to work, Wrike lets you track and see your projects in a way that makes sense for you. Plus, it lets you scale up to manage unlimited projects in one platform, without things getting cluttered. </span></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><b>Wrike has its own intuitive Kanban board view:</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> Track project progress by changing task statuses, just like in Trello. Wrike offers Kanban if that makes sense for you, while letting you chat about entire projects (not just individual tasks). </span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Wrike gives you a </span><b>deeper view of task dependencies with customizable Gantt charts: </b><span style="font-weight: 400;">When Kanban isn’t enough, switch over to Wrike’s </span><a href="https://www.wrike.com/features/gantt-chart/"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Gantt Chart view</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">. This gives you a complete understanding of task dependencies, milestones, and due dates. While Trello is primarily a task management tool, Wrike gives you insight into your entire project.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Wrike </span><b>provides user-friendly, personalized dashboards for every single colleague:</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> Every team member has their own workflows and to-do lists. With Wrike’s </span><a href="https://www.wrike.com/features/dashboards/"><span style="font-weight: 400;">dashboards</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">, they can immediately get visibility on their own priorities, checklists, notifications, tasks, and subtasks, within the context of your whole organization’s work. </span></li>
</ul>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Why choose Wrike over Trello? Wrike offers greater governance, power, and oversight than Trello, making it suitable for teams that need robust reporting and structure.</span></p>
<h3><b>Asana: Best for scaling teams</b></h3>
<p><a href="https://asana.com/" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Asana</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> is one of the best-known project management platforms, designed to help team collaboration by organizing work in a single connected space. It’s used by millions of people worldwide and has 300+ ready-made integrations. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Where Asana particularly shines is in cross-functional work. Portfolio views let managers track progress across multiple projects simultaneously, and goal tracking ties day-to-day tasks to company objectives. Both are available on the Advanced plan.</span></p>
<p><b>Good fit if your team needs:</b></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Automated workflows and dependency tracking across complex projects</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Customizable intake forms that route and assign work automatically</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Portfolio and goal tracking across multiple teams</span></li>
</ul>
<p><b>Not the right call if you need:</b></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Native time tracking without upgrading to Advanced</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Advanced reporting and workload management on a starter budget</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">A simpler interface for teams managing high task volumes</span></li>
</ul>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Ultimately, many users choose </span><a href="https://www.wrike.com/compare/wrike-vs-asana/"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Wrike over Asana</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> and Trello because it offers a less cluttered user experience, increased visibility, and more powerful work management features. </span></p>
<p><a href="https://asana.com/pricing" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Asana’s paid plans</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> start at $10.99 per user per month, billed annually, or $13.49 per user per month billed monthly.</span></p>
<h3><b>ClickUp: Best broad functionality</b></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Another of the most popular project planning tools, </span><a href="https://clickup.com/" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span style="font-weight: 400;">ClickUp</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> is often cited as a strong option for </span><a href="https://www.wrike.com/kanban-guide/kanban-tools-software/" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Kanban board software</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">. It has solutions for many different use cases, offering automations, chat tools, templates, and more.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">When </span><a href="https://www.wrike.com/compare/wrike-vs-clickup/"><span style="font-weight: 400;">comparing ClickUp</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> to other work management platforms, many users focus on proofing and approval capabilities. Trello allows users to add proofs to boards via a plug-in called PageProof, and ClickUp lets users mark changes or leave comments on specific file types. </span></p>
<p><b>Good fit if your team needs:</b></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">An all-in-one workspace with tasks, docs, chat, and whiteboards</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">15+ project views, including Gantt, Mind Map, and workload</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Extensive workflow and dashboard customization</span></li>
</ul>
<p><b>Not the right call if you need:</b></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">A simple tool with a low learning curve</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Reliable performance on large projects</span></li>
</ul>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">ClickUp offers a “free forever” plan with basic features such as Kanban boards. It also has three paid plans starting at $7 per user per month (billed annually).</span></p>
<h3><b>Jira: Best for development teams</b></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Jira Software, Jira Core, and Jira Work Management are all tools that help businesses and organizations manage their work and collaborate with colleagues. While Trello is aimed at all kinds of teams, </span><a href="https://www.atlassian.com/software/jira" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Jira</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> is primarily for software developers and </span><a href="https://www.wrike.com/use-cases/agile-project-management/"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Agile project management</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Many people don’t realize that Trello and Jira are actually owned by the same company, the Atlassian Group,  which provides similar functionality across many of its products. Both use a Kanban board as their main project view, but the level of complexity differs significantly: Trello is easier to use, but Jira offers much more context into project and task details. </span></p>
<p><b>Good fit if your team needs:</b></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Scrum and Kanban workflows that are built around sprints and epics</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Deep integration with development tools like GitHub and Bitbucket</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Advanced Agile reporting, including </span><a href="https://www.wrike.com/blog/what-is-a-velocity-chart-in-agile/"><span style="font-weight: 400;">velocity charts</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> and burndowns</span></li>
</ul>
<p><b>Not the right call if you need:</b></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">A tool for non-technical or cross-functional teams</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">A straightforward setup without significant configuration</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Built-in time tracking or </span><a href="https://www.wrike.com/use-cases/resource-management/"><span style="font-weight: 400;">resource management</span></a></li>
</ul>
<h3><b>Monday.com: Best use case templates</b></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">If you’re looking for a </span><a href="https://www.wrike.com/kanban-guide/kanban-tools-software/"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Kanban tool</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">, </span><a href="http://monday.com/" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Monday.com</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> will probably appear on your radar. It’s one of the better-known work management solutions on the market. It has a wide range of features such as dashboards, automations, Gantt charts, and Kanban boards. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Where Trello and Monday.com differ is in the range of their offering. Monday.com includes more work management capabilities, such as resource management, in addition to the task management features Trello provides. It also offers a large number of ready-made templates for a variety of use cases.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">While Trello’s views, templates, and budgeting plugins help users visualize and organize their resourcing, Monday.com’s features offer greater configurability, including time tracking and a column for assigning project tasks to people.</span></p>
<p><b>Good fit if your team needs:</b></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Visually intuitive boards with 27+ views, including Gantt and timeline</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Highly customizable workflows with minimal technical setup</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Strong integrations across common business tools</span></li>
</ul>
<p><b>Not the right call if you need:</b></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><a href="https://www.wrike.com/price/"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Predictable pricing at scale</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">  (seats are sold in blocks, not individually)</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Native timesheets or advanced time tracking</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Automation without hitting limits on lower-tier plans</span></li>
</ul>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">For a complete resource management solution, </span><a href="https://www.wrike.com/compare/monday-alternative/"><span style="font-weight: 400;">compare Wrike’s offering to Monday.com’s</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">.</span></p>
<h3><b>Notion: Best for documentation-heavy teams </b></h3>
<p><a href="https://www.notion.com/" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Notion</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> is not a traditional project management tool, but a lot of teams use it as one. The appeal is that your tasks and context live in the same place. Instead of managing a board in one tab and your documentation in another, Notion pulls them together through pages and databases that you can shape into Kanban boards, project timelines, wikis, or whatever structure your team needs.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Unfortunately, Notion does not come with a </span><a href="https://www.wrike.com/blog/project-management-template/"><span style="font-weight: 400;">ready-made project management structure</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">, so your experience depends entirely on how well your system is designed. Teams that invest time in getting that right tend to love it. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Reporting and workload visibility are limited compared to dedicated PM tools. If complex, multi-stakeholder projects are the norm for your team, Notion might frustrate you. If documentation and </span><a href="https://www.wrike.com/use-cases/project-tracking-software/"><span style="font-weight: 400;">project tracking</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> carry equal weight and you want both without paying for two tools, it is worth serious consideration.</span></p>
<p><b>Good fit if your team needs:</b></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Kanban boards, timelines, and wikis in one place</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">A single tool for both project tracking and documentation</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Flexibility to build your own workflow structure</span></li>
</ul>
<p><b>Not the right call if you need:</b></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Reporting and workload visibility across complex projects</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Resource planning or multi-stakeholder tracking</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">A plug-and-play PM platform that needs minimal setup</span></li>
</ul>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Pricing starts at $10 per user per month, with a free tier for individuals.</span></p>
<h3><b>Airtable: Best for database-style PM</b></h3>
