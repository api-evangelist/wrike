<h2>What is Trello?</h2>
<p>Trello, at first glance, is an interactive Kanban board that fuels team productivity through straightforward drag-and-drop cards and action item lists. Yet the tool offers much more than just a minimalist method for collaboration.</p>
<p>Beneath the surface, you’ll find that Trello empowers team members with a range of integrations, customizable workspaces, and various templates.</p>
<p>As a Wrike alternative that works best for small businesses, Trello can help streamline organization-wide project management. While it may not work as well for the burgeoning business or enterprise, Trello excels in the hands of individual departments or smaller teams.</p>
<p>It’s best used as a simple pipeline-tracking project management dashboard. If you run a content production agency, for example, you can use Trello to visualize each blog post or article as it moves through your pipeline. The first list could represent the brief drawn up for the article, the next could indicate when the writing work is in progress, and then you could account for the editing or publication processes with respective lists.</p>
<p>With a system like this, you can streamline your workflow so that each team member knows exactly what they need to work on at all times.</p>
<p>Here are the main features of Trello’s project management software:</p>
<ul>
<li>Cloud-based tools</li>
<li>Kanban board organization</li>
<li>Drag-and-drop cards and lists </li>
<li>Mentions, labels, and comments</li>
<li>Custom due dates</li>
<li>Checklists and attachments</li>
<li>Power-ups (third-party integrations)</li>
<li>Workflow automation</li>
<li>Data dashboard</li>
<li>Multiple views</li>
<li>Templates</li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Trello Inbox (a place to capture tasks from email and Slack before sorting them onto your boards).</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Trello Planner</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">AI features (automating workflows, summarizing content, and speeding up project planning)</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Mirror cards</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Butler automation</span></li>
</ul>
<h2>What are the most popular features of Trello?</h2>
<p>Trello might seem like a bare-bones project management solution, but the few things it does, it does well. Here are the most popular features of the team productivity platform:</p>
<h3>Custom workspace boards</h3>
<p>It isn’t uncommon to have various projects ongoing at once, and it’s likely that some tasks and responsibilities overlap. That’s why Trello offers custom workspace boards, so team leaders can quickly navigate from one project to another while seeing how everything fits together with the calendar and table overviews.</p>
<p>If you have multiple projects running at the same time and various task dependencies, Trello’s workspace boards can help you recognize when the dominoes start to fall. You can also bring all the information together, so you have an all-in-one view that encompasses everything.</p>
<h3>Drag-and-drop cards</h3>
<p>Drag-and-drop cards are the lifeblood of Trello. They’re easy to move and as useful for the project manager as they are for team members. Workspace boards are for the big-picture elements of project management, while cards represent the day-to-day tasks and responsibilities. Users can easily create custom cards and do the following:</p>
<ul>
<li>Assign team members</li>
<li>Set due dates</li>
<li>Attach files</li>
<li>Provide checklists</li>
<li>Add comments</li>
</ul>
<h3>Power-ups</h3>
<p>What Trello may lack in functionality outside of task management, it makes up for with what it calls “power-ups.” Projects require more than just productivity, which is why Trello partners with a range of third-party tools to offer many types of integrations, including the following:</p>
<ul>
<li>Analytics and reporting</li>
<li>Automation</li>
<li>File management</li>
<li>HR and operations</li>
<li>Marketing and social media</li>
<li>Sales and support</li>
</ul>
<p>With these integrations, Trello can become an all-in-one solution for your project management needs. Syncing information across apps provides a streamlined way to run your projects from start to finish.</p>
<p>This is useful, especially if you already rely on many apps to run your day-to-day operations. For example, if you use a tool like QuickBooks for your accounting needs, you can feed project information, such as time spent on various tasks, directly into your software. That way, you’re guaranteed accurate billing or payroll.</p>
<h3><b>Butler automation</b></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Repetitive tasks eat into team productivity more than most people realize. Butler is Trello’s built-in automation tool that handles the manual work for you, using a no-code interface that anyone on the team can set up. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">You can create rules that trigger automatic actions, such as moving a card to a new list, assigning a team member, or setting a due date, all without lifting a finger. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Scheduled commands keep things running in the background, too, like sending a weekly reminder every Monday morning. However, with just 250 command runs a month, active teams could find themselves exhausting their Butler execution limits quickly, forcing them to move to Premium and Enterprise plans.</span></p>
<h3><b>Custom fields</b></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Every team tracks different information, and Trello’s custom fields let you tailor cards to reflect exactly what your team needs to see. Whether that’s a project status, budget figure, priority level, or any other data point specific to your workflow, you can add it directly to a card.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Custom fields are not available in Trello’s Free plan; they require a Standard, Premium, or Enterprise subscription.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Other popular features include:</span></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><b>Trello Inbox: </b><span style="font-weight: 400;">A personal space to capture tasks from Slack, Teams, and email (AI-powered summaries are available in paid plans).</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><b>Trello Planner:</b> An integrated scheduling companion; paid plans allow users to drag-and-drop tasks to block out focus time.</li>
</ul>
<h2>Advantages of using Trello</h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Teams that use Trello appreciate the software’s simplicity and ease of setup. Within minutes, you can put together a plan for an upcoming project, break it down into various tasks, and assign those individual tasks to different team members.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">While Trello has expanded its feature set to include Timeline, Table, and Dashboard views in its paid plans, its core user experience remains optimized for its signature </span><b>Kanban-style task management.</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> It distills the process and makes it easy for teams, large and small, to get started on projects quickly.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Beyond basic task management, Trello </span><b>allows teams to collaborate effectively. </b><span style="font-weight: 400;">Through mentions, comments, checklists, and attachments, </span><a href="https://www.wrike.com/project-management-guide/faq/how-to-become-a-project-manager/"><span style="font-weight: 400;">project managers</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> can keep an open line of communication with their team from start to finish. Custom due dates help team members stay on track with their workload and ensure everyone is accountable.</span></p>
<p><b>Checklists and attachments can add another layer of oversight</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> for project managers. If a task requires several steps or reference documentation, they can see to it that the respective cards reflect the relevant information. There’s no need to send team members a list of responsibilities or share information through a file-sharing platform.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">While there’s a </span><b>simple visual timeline of progress</b><span style="font-weight: 400;">, Trello also shows how your team is doing with the cards. Cards stack up in different lists, which you can create to represent different phases of completion. That way, you know exactly what’s left to do and who’s responsible for which tasks.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Finally, </span><b>workflow automation</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> saves you time as you can create shortcuts for recurring or trivial tasks that eat into team productivity.</span></p>
<h2>Disadvantages of using Trello</h2>
<p>Trello’s simplicity is absolutely a strength and makes it an excellent choice for freelancers, contractors, and small teams. The flip side of this is that for larger teams and enterprise-level organizations, it can be <a href="https://www.g2.com/products/trello/reviews?filters%5Bsentiment_snippet%5D=1318958&#038;qs=pros-and-cons#reviews">challenging to manage dozens of projects</a> and hundreds of employees with this project management tool, which is why people often seek <a href="https://www.wrike.com/blog/trello-alternatives/">Trello alternatives</a>.</p>
<p>Another drawback <a href="https://www.capterra.com/p/211559/Trello/reviews/">commonly cited by users</a> is that, at times, when overseeing various workspace boards, pertinent information can get lost in a deluge of notifications. When individual updates come from many different sources, project progress can be hindered as team leaders struggle to stay on top of every board, list, and team member.</p>
