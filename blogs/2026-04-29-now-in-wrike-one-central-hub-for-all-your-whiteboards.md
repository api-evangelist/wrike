<h2><b>Finding your whiteboards just got a whole lot easier</b></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The </span><b>Whiteboards</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> tab removes a small but real source of friction. Instead of remembering where a board lives or @mentioning a teammate in task comments to reshare it, you open a single view, and everything is there.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Click </span><b>Whiteboards</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> in the sidebar, and you’ll immediately see:</span></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">The boards that have been shared with you</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">The boards you’ve recently opened</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">The spaces, projects, tasks, or folders these boards currently belong to</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">The option to create a new board from scratch</span></li>
</ul>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Effective visual collaboration starts with one click! Your </span><a href="https://klaxoon.com/insight/what-is-an-online-whiteboard/" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span style="font-weight: 400;">online whiteboards</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> are exactly where you’d expect them to be, and you can easily filter them by clicking on the columns’ titles.</span></div>

<h3><span style="font-weight: 400;">Simplified visual collaboration for cross-functional teams</span></h3>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Cross-functional teams often juggle multiple — and, let’s be honest, complex — </span><a href="https://www.wrike.com/use-cases/workflow-management-software/"><span style="font-weight: 400;">workflows</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">, each with its own set of visual assets. A design team might run a brainstorming board while product managers maintain a roadmap canvas and engineers sketch out architecture flowcharts. When those boards are scattered across different folders and spaces, people lose track of what exists and where it lives.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The </span><b>Whiteboards</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> tab in Wrike solves this by serving as a unique access point that spans team boundaries. Everyone sees the boards shared with them in a single view, regardless of which team created them. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Focus on speed, not on product frictions. When access is that simple, staying aligned across departments happens naturally.</span></p>
<h2><b>How to create a new whiteboard for every need</b></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Now, there are two ways to create a new whiteboard in your Wrike workspace:</span></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">First way (more specific): Within a space, click on </span><b>Tools</b><span style="font-weight: 400;">, then select </span><b>Whiteboard</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> to get started. Note that only space admins can create whiteboards directly in a space.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Second way (more centralized): Click </span><b>Whiteboards</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> in the sidebar, then hit the </span><b>+ Whiteboard</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> button above the list.</span></li>
</ul>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">If you choose the second option, a pop-up window will let you name and add your new whiteboard to your spaces, projects, folders, or tasks. If added to spaces, whiteboards will appear in the </span><b>Tools</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> section of your sidebar. However, if added to tasks, projects, or folders, they will be attached as files.</span></p>
