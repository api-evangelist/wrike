<h2>What is online proofing software?</h2>
<p>Online proofing software (sometimes called digital proofing or an online proofing tool) helps creative teams and marketers share, request, and gather feedback to maintain expectations and the quality of the work. Instead of endless email exchanges and file attachments, proofing software provides a central platform where all feedback is curated, reviewed, and accepted or rejected.</p>
<p>Feedback is provided directly for each asset, making it clear where creative teams need to improve. Managers and stakeholders can approve the assets that meet all requirements and mark specific areas for changes in those that need more work.</p>
<figure class="custom-image custom-image--outline"><img alt="Product screenshot comparing versions in a proofing tool." class="custom-image-override  aligncenter" height="512" src="https://web-static.wrike.com/blog/content/uploads/www_blogv3/htdocs/content/uploads/2022/10/product-screenshot-proofing-compare-versions.jpg" width="704" /></figure>
<p>Ultimately, online proofing software simplifies the entire review cycle by centralizing the way files and content are shared for approval. It is an essential asset for marketing teams, creative agencies, designers, and video production studios looking to eliminate bottlenecks.</p>
<h2><b>What to look for in an online proofing tool</b></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Choosing the best online proofing software for your business should depend on the business’s needs. There are two types of online proofing software: </span></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Basic proofing software</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Proofing software with task/project management functionalities</span></li>
</ul>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Basic proofing software simply helps teams proof, review, and gather feedback as needed. Proofing software with</span> <a href="https://www.wrike.com/use-cases/task-management/"><span style="font-weight: 400;">task management</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> functionalities enables teams to turn the feedback and comments into actionable tasks they assign to team members and track until completion. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">If you’re a solo creative working with individual clients and a few stakeholders, a simple proofing software solution may be adequate for your needs. However, if you’re an active marketing team, agency, or design studio, you’ll be better served by proofing software with task and project management features. This helps you stay organized and manage multiple projects without dropping the ball while </span><a href="https://www.wrike.com/collaborative-work-guide/faq/how-to-increase-collaboration-between-teams/"><span style="font-weight: 400;">enhancing collaboration</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> with your teams and stakeholders.</span></p>

<h2><b>Benefits of using an online proofing tool</b></h2>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Getting a file from first draft to final approval shouldn’t feel like a game of telephone. The case for digital proofing software comes down to reducing the friction between a file and its final approval. Key benefits include:</span></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><b>Saving time: </b><span style="font-weight: 400;">Online proofing software saves time that would have been spent sending and following up with email conversations to get a file reviewed. Instead of having the same conversation with multiple stakeholders in various email inboxes, proofing software provides a digital space where everyone can share their thoughts.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><b>Organization: </b><span style="font-weight: 400;">Some online proofing tools also provide project management capabilities to help teams create and </span><a href="https://www.wrike.com/webinars/creating-and-using-custom-workflows/"><span style="font-weight: 400;">customize workflows</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">, organize projects into folders and subfolders, and automate file sharing with involved stakeholders — making the entire process more efficient.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><b>Version control: </b><span style="font-weight: 400;">Most proofing tools name and order shared files by the number of revisions, adding details such as timestamps and author information. This makes it easy for the team to know the latest file version and who has worked on it, without wasting time or effort.</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><b>Visibility:</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> Proofing software typically provides status updates for each file. Collaborators can see which are yet to be reviewed or have been approved. Some tools have tracking features that let you know when recipients receive, open, and leave feedback in a file. </span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><b>Accountability: </b><span style="font-weight: 400;">Working with proofing software increases team transparency and accountability. These tools make clear whose absence or lack of contributions is holding up a project, allowing others to nudge or cover for them to keep the project on track. </span></li>
</ul>
<p><b>Other benefits of proofing software are: </b></p>
<ul>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Sending automated reminders to reviewers</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Enabling access to past feedback to improve future projects</span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Providing a centralized workspace for </span><a href="https://www.wrike.com/features/whiteboard/"><span style="font-weight: 400;">brainstorming solutions</span></a></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Providing a stable work environment for creative teams </span><a href="https://www.wrike.com/remote-work-guide/"><span style="font-weight: 400;">working remotely</span></a></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Saving costs that would have been spent subscribing to multiple apps, e.g., communication, file sharing, project management, and cloud storage apps </span></li>
<li style="font-weight: 400;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Increasing chances that the final product meets every stakeholder’s expectations</span></li>
</ul>
